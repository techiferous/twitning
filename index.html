Part I

About Twitning Talks

About Twitning Talks

  • Twitning is a portmanteau of Twitter and lightning.
  • Twitning talks are lightning talks that must not exceed 1:40.
  • They are inspired by Ignite presentations.
Disclaimer: Twitning talks are not affiliated with Twitter.

About Twitning Talks

  • A twitning talk consists of 10 slides.
  • Each slide autoadvances every 10 seconds.

Part II

Creating a Twitning Talk

Creating a Twitning Talk

  • To create a twitning talk, fork this code on github.
  • Replace the contents of index.html with the contents of your slideshow.
  • This code comes with a default CSS theme, basic.css.
  • Add your own style tweaks to custom.css. Or you can create a completely new CSS theme.

Creating a Twitning Talk

  • This slideshow is only targeted at Chrome and Firefox. It works reasonably well in Safari and Opera, but Safari does not have fullscreen mode.
  • Your presentation should actually have twelve slides: a title slide, ten presentation slides, and an ending slide. When the ending slide displays, your time is up (you do not have 10 seconds for the ending slide).
  • Avoid putting content on the bottom third of the screen. This bullet point is too low and much of the audience won't be able to read it.
